# Enterprise AI Decision Framework

---

## Architecture Context

This document explains how ONE COMPANION evaluates, validates, prioritizes, and governs every AI-generated action through the **Enterprise AI Decision Framework**.

Rather than allowing AI to execute recommendations independently, every proposed action is assessed using customer intelligence, enterprise policies, business objectives, risk evaluation, compliance requirements, and governance mechanisms before execution.

The Enterprise AI Decision Framework serves as the enterprise governance layer that ensures every AI-driven interaction remains intelligent, responsible, explainable, secure, and compliant with banking standards.

---

# 1. What is the Enterprise AI Decision Framework?

The Enterprise AI Decision Framework is responsible for evaluating whether a proposed AI action should be executed, modified, delayed, escalated, or rejected.

The Enterprise AI Intelligence Layer generates intelligence.

Agentic AI Orchestration coordinates workflows.

The Enterprise AI Decision Framework validates whether the proposed action is appropriate from a business, customer, security, compliance, and enterprise perspective before execution.

Rather than replacing AI reasoning, the framework ensures every AI recommendation becomes a trusted enterprise decision.

# 4. Enterprise Decision Factors

Every proposed action is evaluated using multiple enterprise intelligence dimensions.

| Decision Factor | Purpose |
|-----------------|---------|
| **Customer Context** | Understand customer behaviour, financial goals, preferences, and current financial situation. |
| **Enterprise Context** | Consider business priorities, product availability, campaigns, operational objectives, and organizational strategy. |
| **Business Policies** | Ensure alignment with enterprise banking rules and operational standards. |
| **Risk Intelligence** | Evaluate customer, financial, operational, and enterprise risk before execution. |
| **Compliance Requirements** | Verify regulatory, legal, and organizational compliance. |
| **Decision Confidence** | Measure the overall reliability of the proposed decision using AI intelligence, governance validation, and enterprise policies. |
| **Customer Impact** | Estimate expected customer value and long-term relationship benefits. |

Together these factors produce balanced enterprise decisions rather than AI-only recommendations.

---

# 5. Enterprise Decision Intelligence Capabilities

The framework performs six core enterprise capabilities.

### Context Evaluation

Combines Customer360° intelligence with the current business situation to establish complete decision context.

---

### Priority Assessment

Determines the urgency, business value, and customer impact of competing recommendations to ensure the most valuable actions are executed first.

---

### Enterprise Validation

Validates proposed actions against enterprise policies, governance standards, operational constraints, and business rules.

---

### Risk Evaluation

Assesses financial, operational, security, and customer risk before approving any AI-driven action.

---

### Decision Governance

Approves, modifies, postpones, escalates, or rejects proposed actions according to enterprise evaluation results.

---

### Continuous Learning

Monitors execution outcomes and customer responses to continuously improve future enterprise decision quality.

---

# 6. Enterprise Decision Outcomes

Every evaluated recommendation results in one of five enterprise outcomes.

| Decision Outcome | Description |
|------------------|-------------|
| **Approve** | Execute the recommendation immediately. |
| **Modify** | Adjust the recommendation before execution. |
| **Delay** | Postpone execution until appropriate conditions are met. |
| **Escalate** | Forward the recommendation for human review or approval. |
| **Reject** | Prevent execution because enterprise validation requirements are not satisfied. |

This structured outcome model ensures every AI-driven action follows a controlled and explainable enterprise decision process.

---

# 7. Decision Governance Principles

Every enterprise decision follows six governance principles.

### Customer-First Decisions

Every validated action must create measurable value for the customer.

---

### Explainable Decisions

Every enterprise decision must remain transparent, traceable, auditable, and explainable.

---

### Policy-Driven Intelligence

Enterprise policies always take precedence over AI-generated recommendations.

---

### Risk-Aware Governance

Customer protection, operational stability, financial security, and regulatory compliance remain central to every decision.

---

### Human Oversight

High-risk, exceptional, or sensitive decisions can be escalated for human review whenever required.

---

### Continuous Improvement

Every completed decision contributes new enterprise intelligence that continuously improves future decision quality.

- Increase consistency across intelligent banking services.

- Build a trusted governance foundation for enterprise-scale AI adoption.
